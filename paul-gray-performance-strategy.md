## The Core Insight

Kids arriving at learn-to-swim programs and entry-level squads today are, on average,
weaker, less mobile, and less fit than kids were a generation ago. This isn't a swimming
problem — it's an upstream physical foundation problem that shows up *in* swimming
(and other sports) because swimming is often a kid's first structured physical activity.

**The consequence in practice:**
- Lessons get spent building basic body capability instead of teaching technique
- Kids progress slower through learn-to-swim and squad pathways
- The foundation gap creates downstream injury risk and limits sport options later

**The opportunity:** Build the foundational physical layer *before* or *alongside* swim
teaching — strength, mobility, fitness — so that by the time a kid is in lessons or squads,
their body is already capable, and the lesson time goes to actual technique.

This reframes the business from "swim coach" to **"the foundational layer for kids' sport
performance, taught through swimming and movement."** Swimming is the entry vehicle, not
the whole product.

## Positioning Statement (working draft)

> Most kids today are weaker and less mobile than they should be. It slows down how they
> learn to swim, limits how they perform in any sport, and raises injury risk later.
> Paul Gray Performance builds the physical foundation first — strength, mobility,
> fitness — so swimming (and everything after it) comes easier, faster, and safer.

This is the lead message for the homepage. Parents don't need the business model
explained to them — they need the problem named in a way that clicks, and the solution
made obvious.

## Business Model — Tiered Progression

The business is structured in ascending tiers. Lower tiers build trust, audience, and
top-of-funnel reach. Higher tiers generate revenue and the actual performance data.

### Tier 1 — Free Entry Point
- **Mechanism:** Email signup on the website
- **Deliverable:** Free foundational PDF guide (see spec below)
- **Purpose:** Capture interest, build email list, establish credibility and philosophy
  before any payment is asked for

### Tier 2 — Low-Cost Community / Foundational Program
- **Mechanism:** Paid community (Skool or similar platform) and/or a structured entry-level
  online course
- **Price point:** Deliberately below the cost of a standard learn-to-swim class —
  accessible, not a barrier
- **Content:** Ongoing foundational exercises, philosophy, basic guidance, community
  support, possibly drip-fed content as it's developed alongside Paul's qualifications
- **Purpose:** Build a engaged, paying audience; create the top of the value ladder; this
  is where most families will sit most of the time

### Tier 3 — Premium Services (revenue + data generation)
- **Mechanism:** Private 1-on-1 coaching, school holiday camps/intensives, workshops
- **Purpose:** Highest revenue per family, and this is where in-person data collection
  happens — FMS screening, swim metrics, strength/mobility tracking
- **Camps specifically:** Positioned as the natural next step for families already in the
  community — an intensive week where foundational work gets put into practice
  in person

### Data Layer (cuts across all tiers, but mainly populated via Tier 3)
- In-person clients (private lessons, camps, workshops): Paul collects metrics directly —
  FMS screening, strength/mobility markers, swim-specific data — and uploads to a tracking
  system
- Online/community-only clients: can self-report data, which gets added manually for now
- **Long-term:** this data populates individual swimmer/athlete profiles, eventually
  surfaced to parents via the paulgray.coach portal (Supabase-backed, future build —
  not Phase 1)
- The qualifications driving this: FMS (Functional Movement Screen), plus swim-specific
  certifications already in progress (SAT, SSF, Development Coach pathway)

## Why This Works As Positioning

- It's **preventative, not reactive** — addressing a root cause rather than competing on
  "better stroke technique" alone
- It's **systemic** — Paul is positioned as someone solving the actual problem (kids are
  unprepared), not just delivering a service (swim lessons)
- It **naturally creates a funnel** — free guide → community → premium → camps, each tier
  logically leading to the next
- It **differentiates from every other swim school** in Sydney, none of which are
  messaging around foundational fitness/mobility as the actual product
- It **future-proofs the brand** beyond swimming — "Paul Gray Performance" as a name
  deliberately doesn't say "swim" because the long-term concept is broader: kids' sport
  performance foundation, with swimming as the current vehicle

## Website Structure (Phase 1 — Keep It to 3 Pages)

### Page 1 — Home / Hero
- **Hero section:** Problem statement + one-sentence solution (see positioning statement
  above) — lead with the kid-weakness insight, not the business model
- **Primary CTA:** "Get the free foundational guide" → email signup → PDF delivery
- **Secondary CTA:** "See how it works" → links to Page 2
- Keep this page short. One clear job: get the email signup.

### Page 2 — How It Works / Approach
- Explain the tier progression in parent-friendly language (free guide → community →
  premium coaching/camps)
- Explain what FMS and metrics actually mean for their kid, in plain terms
- Credentials/qualifications section (SAT, SSF, FMS, Development Coach pathway, etc.) —
  proof and trust signals
- Links out to community/program sign-up when those exist

### Page 3 — Contact / Get Started
- Contact form (for general enquiries — not the email PDF capture, that's on the homepage)
- Could double up as where premium service enquiries land (private lessons, camps,
  workshops) once those are formally offered

**Explicitly NOT for Phase 1:** blog, login portal, course platform, full community
integration. Add these as they're actually built and have content/users.

## PDF Lead Magnet — Spec

The free PDF is the single most important conversion asset on the site. It needs to be
substantive enough that a parent finishes it thinking "that was actually useful," not "that
was just a sales pitch in PDF form."

**Working structure — three parts:**

### Part 1 — Foundational Guide (the bulk of it)
- Basic strength, mobility, and "soft" swimming-adjacent skills kids can build at home or
  poolside
- Practical, age-appropriate exercises — not clinical, not overwhelming
- Should give a parent something they can actually *do* with their kid this week

### Part 2 — Philosophy & Motivation
- Why this matters (the foundational gap problem, explained simply for a parent audience)
- **Critical inclusion: how to make this stuff land with kids.** Structure alone doesn't
  work if a kid isn't engaged — needs a motivation/framing angle.
  - Example already identified: reframing a lap as a "treasure hunt" or "crossing to an
    island" rather than "swim to the other side" — connecting the task to a kid's
    imagination rather than presenting it as exercise
  - This section should give parents a few reusable framing techniques they can apply
    themselves, not just one example

### Part 3 — Community Sampler
- A taste of what the paid community/Tier 2 program offers
- Should demonstrate value without giving away the full program — e.g., "this is the kind
  of small-issue troubleshooting and ongoing support you get inside the community"
- Soft CTA into Tier 2 sign-up at the end

**Target length:** Roughly 5 pages — long enough to be useful, short enough to be read in
one sitting.
